All about CLAT 2025: Eligibility, Syllabus, and Exam Pattern

The Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) is a pivotal examination for aspiring law students in India. As we approach CLAT 2025, it's crucial for candidates to be well-informed about the eligibility criteria, syllabus, and exam pattern. This comprehensive guide is designed to assist you in navigating through the essentials of CLAT 2025, ensuring you're thoroughly prepared for this significant step in your legal career.

Understanding CLAT 2025

Eligibility Criteria for CLAT 2025

Educational Qualifications

    1. For UG Program: Candidates must have completed 10+2 or an equivalent examination with a minimum percentage (as specified by the participating universities, usually around 45-50%).
    2. For PG Program: A Bachelor's degree in Law (LL.B) is required. The minimum percentage criteria, as set by the respective universities, also apply here.
    3. Age Limit: There is no upper age limit for CLAT 2025.

Key Highlights of CLAT Syllabus

For Undergraduate Program

    1. English Language: Comprehension passages, grammar, word meanings, and incorrect/correct sentences.
    2. Current Affairs, including General Knowledge: Current events, arts and culture, world affairs, historical events.
    3. Legal Reasoning: Legal matters, problem-solving questions, legal propositions.
    4. Logical Reasoning: Logical links, analogies, identifying arguments, conclusions.
    5. Quantitative Techniques: Elementary mathematics, graphs, numerical information.

For Postgraduate Program

    • The syllabus includes Constitutional Law, Jurisprudence, and other law subjects such as Contract, Torts, Criminal Law, International Law, IPR, and Family Law.

Exam Pattern for CLAT 2025

For UG Program

    1. Type of Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).
    2. Total Questions: 150.
    3. Duration: 2 hours.
    4. Marking Scheme: +1 for correct answers, -0.25 for incorrect answers.

For PG Program

    1. Type of Questions: MCQs and Subjective.
    2. Total Questions: 120 MCQs and two essay type questions.
    3. Duration: 2 hours.
    4. Marking Scheme: Similar negative marking as UG.

FAQs About CLAT 2025

Q: Can I apply for CLAT 2025 after completing my 10+2 in 2024?

A: Yes, if you meet the percentage criteria set by the participating universities.

Q: Is there a limit to the number of attempts for CLAT?

A: No, there is no limit on the number of attempts as long as you fulfill the eligibility criteria.

Q: Are the CLAT scores valid for all law universities in India?

A: CLAT scores are accepted by National Law Universities (NLUs) and several other law schools in India.

Q: Can I take CLAT 2025 in an online mode?

A: The mode of examination (online/offline) is usually announced by the CLAT Consortium closer to the exam date.

Conclusion

CLAT 2025 stands as a gateway to prestigious law schools and a promising legal career in India. Understanding the eligibility criteria, syllabus, and exam pattern is fundamental in strategizing your preparation. Start early, stay updated with current affairs, and practice consistently to enhance your chances of acing the exam.
